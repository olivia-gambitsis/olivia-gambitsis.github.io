My Learning Plan

My Long Term Goal/s

My long term goal is to one day have the skills to apply for jobs that would allow me to work as a programmer overseas or in jobs where I have the ability to work remotely. After a few years working as a Pharmacist I decided I wanted to travel and work overseas. However, my degree was only recognised in New Zealand and I couldn't practice anywhere else without studying again in said country. I would also like to potentially one day combine my pharmacy knowledge and programming knowledge to improve access to healthcare.

Skills I would like to develop (non-technical - human skills) while at EDA?

I would like develop better communication skills and patience. I have a tendency to be really impatient if I am explaining things or when I am in a stressful situation. This is definitely a limitation I have when it comes to working in a team.

How I will manage my workload during this programme

Coming from working full time, I have come into this programme with the mindset that it is a full time job. I was working six days a week before starting this programme. I believe the best way for me to manage the workload is to continue with same schedule as I did when I was working 8 hours a day. During foundations I intend to study full time Monday to Friday 9:30am to 6:00pm, any time I loose during the day I will make up at night. I intend to only study a few hours on the weekend. When it comes to bootcamp, 70 hours per week is recommended. This is a lot of time to spend studying. I think the best way for me to manage this is to have a plan at the start of each week, including meals, chores, sleep, exercise and fun.

Seeking help

When it comes to seeking help, I think I will always try my best to find the answer on my own first, then reach out to my cohort, and then the facilitators. I would like to be able to come to the facilitators for help if I cannot find the asnwers elsewhere. I also look to the facilitators for constuctive critism on my work.
