Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

If people understand how their brain works and the idea that the brain can adapt and change, it can be more motivating to learn new things, change unhealthy behavoirs, and encourage people to try things they may have thought they weren't "smart" enough or capable enough to do before.

Discuss how might engage with the principals of neuroplasticity for your own benefit

I believe I can benefit from understanding the idea that practicing something over and over again or revising something causes stronger connections between the neurons in your brain. This is good motivation to keep my brain active and to keep going over and practing concepts that I have learnt.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity

Practice and revision are important. Our brain is also better at remembering something if we can associate it with something we already know! This is because we already have strong connnections in the brain for things that we have learnt before. If we can relate the new information to that, then not as many new connections need to be made.

Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant

Growth Mindset is the idea that intelligence is not a “set” value and that one can improve, the brain is not stuck. It is relevant because when an individual knows this, they may be more likely to achieve knowing that their brain can change and they are not “smart” or “dumb”. One of the most important things in developing a growth mindset is embracing challenges. Just because you cannot do something straight away does not mean that you will never be able to do it, it just means that you may need to work a bit harder or change your approach. Another important part of developing a growth mindset is to stop comparing yourself to others, everybody is at different stages and they may have different upbringings and education. It is better to compare your current self to your past self, for example; has your ability to do a certain thing improved compared to how it once was??.

I think as I mentioned above its important that when I find something difficult I need to see it as a challenge, not as a barrier.
